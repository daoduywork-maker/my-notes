    Miscellaneous and frequently asked questions (FAQs)

    This page is dedicated to new undergrads, especially Vietnamese, who aspire to pursue a research career.

    Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)

    I can't stress enough the importance of REU if you want to pursue a research career. In fact, in my experience, REU and recommendation from your principal investigators (PIs) and academic supervisors outweigh your GPA (a competittive GPA still helps even if it's not tantamount). Here are some programs that I have applied. They are all fully-funded and have computational physics/chemistry positions. You can read the information in detail directly at their website. Here, I will just provide the insights to apply for those programs if there are any.

    Germany

    Germany has two prominent research institutes with summer program for international undergraduate students: Helmholtz Zentrum and Max Planck Institute.

    • Helmholtz Zentrum Dresden Rossendorf (HZDR) and Berlin (HZB) Summer Program: Relevancy is key. Supervisors usually choose candidates whose research interest and experience align with them instead of looking at your GPA. My advice is to look through your prefered department, scan through the PIs list, and read their latest researches thoroughly to tailor your profile accordingly. This can actually apply to everywhere and anything.

    • Max Planck Institute: If you don't have a 3.6/4.0, you needn't bother applying for this. If you do, you still need prior experience in your home university's lab to compete. In short, absolute academic excellence is mandatory.

    Finland

    • Aalto Science Institute (AScI) International Summer Research Programme: It's best that you display proficient english during interview since Finnish employers, in general, prefer to work with people whom they can communicate easily. It's very competitive - over 7000 applicants in 2025 and 5000 in 2026. This is the one program that I have to stress how important language proficiency is.

    Taiwan

    • Taiwan International Internship Program (TIIP): Those are essentially pre-doctoral level program. Extensive prior experience is a must.

    • International Internship Piloting Program (IIPP)

    • Taiwan Education Exchance Program (TEEP)

    Japan

    • University of Tokyo Summer Internship Program (UTSIP): A strong GPA is a strong starting point since PIs' requirements on experience for interns are generally low. Regardless, a solid background on the research makes things easier.

    • Univeristy of Tokyo Research Internship Program (UTRIP): Quite literallly the Asian's counterpart of Max Planck institute.

    Graduate Programs and Scholarships

    Here, I only introduce programs for computational physics/chemistry and material science. Again, You can read the information in detail directly at their website. I also want to stress that global ranking doesn't mean everything: It comprises of many metrics, some of which are inconsequential to the actual learning experience and outcomes. The important things are: Stipends, Coursework, and your PIs. All of the programs below are either fully-funded or have external grants that you can apply.

    European Union - Erasmus_Mundus Joint Master Degree (EMJMD)

    You will study in different schools belonged to a consortium in each semester. The stipend is really good of 1400 euros. Since you have to compete globally, the difficulty need not be discussed.

    • EMJMD in Theoretical Chemistry and Computational Modeling (TCCM): This one required extensive coursework including physical chemistry, quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, ... along with background on computational chemistry methods and theories such as Density Functional Theory (DFT), Molecular Dynamics (MD), ... The payoff is that the consortium comprises of prestigious schools in the field and a dream-like cirriculum for theorist.

    • Erasmus-Mundus Master in Nanoscience and Nanotechnology (EMM-NANO): It has a modeling track based in Uiversity of Barcelona. A strong GPA (preferably above 3.2/4.0) with some REUs are sufficient.

    China - China Scholarship Council (CSC)

    • Tsinghua University - International Master Program of Material Science and Engineering (IMP-MSE): A decent GPA and a working knowledge on any fields regarding material science are sufficient. They have a big council for interview: The questions are heavily oriented toward AI integration into material science and authenticating your research experience.

    France

    • Paris Sciences et Lettres (PSL) - PhD Track in Chemistry: Their cirriculum are heavily oriented toward computation and modeling despite a very general name of "Chemistry". An excellent GPA (atleast 3.6/4.0) and extensive coursework in chemistry and physics is a mch. Otherwise, you need not bother applying.

    Taiwan

    • Academia Sinica - Taiwan Internationa Graduate Program: It requires 3 recommnedations. One of them has to be that of your academic supervisor. Participating in TIIP is an advantage since you can get quality recommendations from within the institute.

    Germany - German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD)

    The thing about German graduate program is that: If you come from Vietnam, China, and India, you have to authenticate your diplomas (both bachelor and highschool) through German Embassy to get a certificate, called APS. The authenication process takes very long. There are two cycles per year. You have to submit your documents 3 months prior to the interviews, which take place in May and November, and wait another 1 month to get your certificate. The tricky part is that admission cycles usually end before May and November, so you have to prepare this in advance, preferrably 6 months early.

    • Georg-August-Universität Göttingen - Master in Chemsitry : Its ranking seems normal, but it was quite literally the birth place of quantum mechanics, or modern physics in general: It's where Bohr, Heisenberg, and Oppenheimer worked. I don't think I need to elaborate further.

    • Friedrich-Schiller-Universität Jena - Master in Chemistry

    Hungary - Stipendium Hungaricum

    Hungary is such an underdog - it has a long-standing history of physics and science in general, affordable life, and top-tier institutes. Edward Teller, Leo Szilard, Rudorf Ortvay, John von Neumann, ... are all Hungarian. This scholarship covers almost everything: accomodation, insurance, along with some stipend.

    • Eötvös Loránd University (ELTE) - MSc in Materials Science: ELTE is arguably the top science university in Hungary and has a powerhouse in theoretical and computational chemistry. Its Laboratory of Theoretical Chemistry is one of the most prominent groups in Europe. They are particularly famous for their work in high-accuracy quantum chemistry, development of theoretical methods, and computational spectroscopy.

    • Budapest University of Technology and Economics (BME) - MSc in Materials Engineering

    • University of Debrecen - MSc in Materials Science

    VISA application for REUs

    General tips

    • Please keep any notarized translation of your documents! You may need it for future application. They are unexpectedly expensive.

    • Have a plus-1 of every document that you print - An original one, a copied one, and a back-up one.

    Germany

    For Vietnamese students, following the instruction from the embassy is sufficient. However, you have to start early since it can take up to 1 month to obtain your visa. One important thing is that you have to translate your highschool diploma if it's required, and get it notarized.

    Taiwan

    Of course, It requires your notarized translation of your available diploma, which is highschool one if you are still an undergrad. This time, you need to get that notarized diploma authenticated by the Department of External Affair (6 Alexander de Rhodes street, District 1).

    Moreover, although you will be funded, they still requires a proof of sufficient fund, which comprises of your sponsor's proof of saving account along with any documents that prove your relation with them (usually your birth certificate if your sponsor is your parent).
